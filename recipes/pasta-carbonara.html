Pasta Carbonara

Carbonara is an Italian pasta dish from Rome made with eggs, hard cheese, cured pork and black pepper. The dish arrived at its modern form, with its current name, in the middle of the 20th century. The cheese is usually Pecorino Romano, Parmigiano-Reggiano, or a combination of the two. Spaghetti is the most common pasta, but fettuccine, rigatoni, linguine, or bucatini are also used. Normally guanciale or pancetta are used for the meat component, but lardons of smoked bacon are a common substitute outside Italy.

For decadent, creamy carbonara with lots of Parmesan, crispy fried bacon and a hint of nutmeg, you only need 6 ingredients and 20 minutes.

Ingredients for 2 servings

  • 250 g pasta (e.g. spaghetti)
  • 150 g ham cubes
  • 2 eggs
  • 200 ml cream (or milk)
  • 80 g parmesan
  • some salt, pepper and nutmeg

Preparation

  1. Prepare the pasta:

    Cook pasta al dente according to package directions.

  2. Meanwhile:

    Whisk together eggs, cream and Parmesan. Season with salt, pepper and nutmeg.

  3. It gets creamy!

    Add pasta directly from pasta water to bacon in pan and add egg mixture. Mix everything together, then over very low heat, stirring until the sauce sets. Season again to taste and enjoy.
